Innovation That Includes

Wisdom M. Johnson | Pine Forest High School | Fayetteville, NC
Prompt: What core principles or values do you believe are most essential to creating positive change in your community and future career field? How do you plan to embody them in both your educational pursuits and interactions with others?

The principle I keep coming back to is simple: innovation should never leave people behind. Technology is genuinely powerful, but I think it only matters when it is being used to create real opportunities for people, especially in communities that are not always first in line to benefit from it.

Through the Academy of Information Technology, Upward Bound, and the entrepreneurship courses I have been part of, I started seeing really clearly how connected technology and economic opportunity actually are. It is not just about making things faster or more efficient. It is about improving access. Access to education, to information, to jobs, to resources. The communities that have those things grow. The ones that do not stay stuck. And that gap does not fix itself. Somebody has to decide to close it.

That is what drove me to start WisdomLayers and get involved with Code Forward. I wanted to prove that you do not have to wait for a degree to start building something useful for the people around you. WisdomLayers gave me a way to serve small businesses in Fayetteville that were being priced out of having any real digital presence. Code Forward gave me a way to teach younger students that technology is something they can build with, not just something that happens around them.

Going into engineering, I want to take that same thinking and apply it at a much bigger scale. I want to design solutions with equity built into them from the beginning, not as an afterthought. Because if the work I am doing is only reaching people who already have every advantage, it is not really solving anything. It is just making things more convenient for people who were already fine.

In school, I try to live these values by being a collaborator before I am a competitor. Sharing what I know, lifting the people around me, and measuring my success by what I help others accomplish, not just by what I accomplish on my own. That is how I plan to carry this into every room I walk into. If the work only benefits me, I am not doing it right.
