Deals on the Greens

Wisdom M. Johnson | Pine Forest High School | Fayetteville, NC
Prompt: Do you have any affiliation to golf, to include collegiate or recreational play, work at a course or other related experience?

A mentor told me something a few years back that I genuinely have not stopped thinking about. We were talking about how professionals actually build meaningful relationships, not just through formal meetings or credentials, but in the spaces in between. And he said it plainly: business happens in the boardroom, but deals are made on the greens.

I did not fully grasp it in that moment. But I understood enough to start paying attention. I began noticing which of my peers had experience with golf, through family, through school programs, through just growing up in environments where that was normal. And I noticed that I was not in that group. Golf was not part of how I grew up. I did not have access to lessons or courses or any of that. But instead of seeing that as something to dismiss, I started seeing it as something worth understanding.

Because what my mentor was really saying is that golf is not just a sport. It is a setting. It is the kind of environment where professional relationships are built slowly, over several hours, in a way that a thirty minute meeting simply does not allow. The comfort, the patience, the presence it takes to play a full round with someone, that communicates something that a resume never could. And the people who are at ease in that setting tend to have real advantages in the rooms where things get decided.

So while I do not yet have a handicap or a home course, I am intentional about what comes next. As I move into college I plan to take up golf seriously, the same way I have approached every other skill I have built, whether that was web development or leadership or teaching code to students who had never written a line of it before. I want to walk onto a course with real confidence, not as someone trying to fit in, but as someone who prepared.

The greens are where a certain kind of relationship gets built. I intend to be there, ready to be part of those conversations.
