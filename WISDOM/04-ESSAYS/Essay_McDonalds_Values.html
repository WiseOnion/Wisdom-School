Building the Bridge

Wisdom M. Johnson | Pine Forest High School | Fayetteville, NC
Prompt: What values are most important to you for making a positive difference in your community and future career? How will you show those values in school and when working with others?

I grew up in Fayetteville, North Carolina. It is a military city, and one of the things you absorb pretty early growing up there is that showing up for people matters. Hard work matters. Community matters. Those things got into me before I had words for them. But the moment that really made those values concrete happened when I was working with a small business owner who needed a website.

She had a great product. Her customers loved her. She just had no digital presence and no way to reach anyone who did not already know her. So she reached out to a design agency. They sent her a quote that was completely out of reach for her budget. And I watched her just accept that. Like, okay, I guess this space is not for me.

That bothered me. Because the only thing standing between her and real growth was access. Not talent, not effort, just access. And that is where my values actually come from. I care deeply about equity, integrity, and making sure that the work I do serves people who genuinely need it, not just people who already have everything figured out.

That is what pushed me to start WisdomLayers, my web development company, and to get involved with Code Forward, where I teach young people in my community how to use technology to build things. I did not want to sit around waiting for a degree before I started contributing. I wanted to start now, with the skills I already had.

In school, those values show up in how I treat the people around me. As Secretary of the National Technical Honor Society, I am not just holding a title. I am trying to make sure the students in that program feel genuinely supported. I share what I know. I stay after class. Integrity, to me, means doing that kind of thing consistently, not just in the moments where it is obvious or easy.

Going into engineering, I want to design things the same way I try to live. With real people in mind from the start. Technology that only benefits people who already have every advantage is not really making a difference. It is just making things more comfortable for people who were already comfortable. My goal is to help build something better than that. That woman got her website. I built it. She is still running her business. That is where I want to start. I just want to do it at a much bigger scale.
