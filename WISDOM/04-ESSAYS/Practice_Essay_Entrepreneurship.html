The First Invoice

Wisdom M. Johnson | Pine Forest High School | Fayetteville, NC
Prompt: Describe a time you showed entrepreneurial spirit.

The most honest thing I can say about starting WisdomLayers is that I did not feel ready. I knew how to build websites. I had been learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for two years through the Academy of Information Technology at Pine Forest. I had done class projects, designed layouts, built things from scratch. But working for a real client, someone who is paying you, depending on you, and has no patience for a learning curve, that is a completely different thing.

I started WisdomLayers in June 2025 because I kept noticing the same gap. Small businesses and community organizations in Fayetteville needed a web presence but could not afford design agencies. And students at my school had no idea that web development was something they could turn into an actual business before graduation. I saw both of those problems as one opportunity: build something real, serve real clients, prove that it could be done. I named it WisdomLayers because the work is not just code on a screen. It is layers of decisions about design, user experience, and what someone actually needs when they land on your page.

Getting that first client was the hard part. You cannot show a portfolio when you have no clients, and you cannot get clients without a portfolio. So I did what most people starting out do. I offered to work at a lower rate to build the relationship, delivered something better than they expected, and let the work speak. That first project taught me more than any course had. I learned how to ask the right questions before starting. I learned how to scope a project so both sides know exactly what is being delivered. I learned that done and done well are not the same thing, and that only one of them gets you the next job.

Running a business alongside school, NTHS, varsity tennis, Code Forward, and everything else required a kind of discipline I had not needed before. I had to manage my own schedule, set realistic timelines, and communicate clearly when something was taking longer than expected. Those skills do not show up in a GPA. But they show up in every professional relationship you will ever have.

The most entrepreneurial thing about WisdomLayers is not the technical work. It is the decision to start before I felt ready, and to keep going through the discomfort of not knowing exactly what I was doing yet. Entrepreneurship is not a personality type. It is a choice to treat uncertainty as a workable condition instead of a reason to wait. I made that choice at 17, in Fayetteville, with a laptop and a service worth offering. I intend to keep making it.
