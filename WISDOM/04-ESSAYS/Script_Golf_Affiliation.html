Spoken video script, 2 minutes. The goal is to be honest without being apologetic, and confident without being arrogant. You do not have golf experience yet, and that is fine. What matters is how you frame it. Be genuine. Let the mentor story land before you move on.
0:00 to 0:20 — Just be real

So honestly, no, I have not played golf. That was not really part of my world growing up. But the reason I am not apologetic about that is because not having access to something and not understanding its value are two very different things.

0:20 to 1:00 — The mentor story, let it land

A mentor told me something a few years back that genuinely stuck with me. We were just talking, kind of like this, about how leaders actually build relationships. Not in the formal stuff, but around it. And he said, business happens in the boardroom, but deals are made on the greens.

And I sat with that for a while. Because what he was really saying is that golf is not about the sport. It is about the setting. You are spending four hours with someone. Nobody is performing. Nobody is rushing through an agenda. You are just there, together, and that kind of time builds something that a thirty minute meeting never will. I got that. I really got that.

1:00 to 1:25 — What you noticed after that

After that conversation I started paying more attention. I noticed which of my peers grew up around golf, through family, through school programs, through just having that kind of access. And I noticed I was not in that group. But I did not see that as a reason to write it off. I saw it as something worth being intentional about.

1:25 to 2:00 — Where you are headed

So going into college, I plan to take golf seriously. As a skill, and as a professional tool. I want to be comfortable in those spaces, not faking it, actually comfortable. Because the conversations that shape careers a lot of the time are not happening in formal settings. They are happening exactly where my mentor described. And I want to be there, ready, when they do.
