Innovation That Includes

Wisdom M. Johnson | Pine Forest High School | Fayetteville, NC
Prompt: What core principles or values do you believe are most essential to creating positive change in your community and future career field? How do you plan to embody them in both your educational pursuits and interactions with others?
Spoken video script. Read it like you are talking to someone who genuinely asked you this question. Not a speech, not a presentation. A real conversation. Practice it out loud until you stop sounding like you are reading.

Hi, my name is Wisdom Johnson. I am a senior at Pine Forest High School in Fayetteville, North Carolina, and I truly appreciate the opportunity to be considered for this.

Honestly, I believe one of the most important principles for creating positive change is using innovation with purpose. Technology is powerful, but I think it matters most when it is used ethically and in ways that create real opportunities for people, especially in underserved communities.

From my experience in the Academy of Information Technology and Upward Bound, I started to see how technology and entrepreneurship can work together to drive economic growth. For me, that means creating solutions that do not just make processes faster, but actually improve access. Whether that is access to education, information, jobs, or resources.

A perspective that really defines me is that innovation should never leave people behind. That is actually what led me to found WisdomLayers, my web development company, where I manage a small team of individuals who build websites and digital tools for small businesses and community organizations that are often priced out of having a real online presence. I want to use technology in a way that promotes equity, so communities that are often overlooked can still benefit from digital growth and new economic opportunities.

That same thinking is what got me involved with Code Forward, where I teach younger students in my community that technology is something they can build with, not just something that happens around them. I did not want to wait for a degree to start making a difference. But I recognize that the education, the community, the tools, and the mentorship that come with a degree will only help me go further for the people around me.

As I continue my education, I plan to combine engineering and IT to build ethical, community-centered solutions that help close opportunity gaps, create jobs, and empower people to succeed. I want my future career to be about more than personal success. I want it to be about building systems that help others rise too.

Thank you so much for your time and attention. This opportunity genuinely means a lot.
