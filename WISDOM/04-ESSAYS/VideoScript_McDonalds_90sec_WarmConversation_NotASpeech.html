Building the Bridge

Wisdom M. Johnson | Pine Forest High School | Fayetteville, NC
Prompt: What values are most important to you for making a positive difference in your community and future career? How will you show those values in school and when working with others? (McDonald's Scholarship Video Response)
Spoken video script. Keep it warm, keep it real. You are not giving a speech. You are having a conversation. Aim for 90 seconds to 2 minutes. Practice out loud, not just in your head.
0:00 to 0:20 — Start with what you actually believe

The values that matter most to me are equity, integrity, and community. And I do not say those as abstract ideas. For me they are very practical. They show up in the decisions I make every day about who I help, how I do my work, and what I am building toward.

0:20 to 0:55 — Tell the story that explains it

I grew up in Fayetteville, which is a military city, and hard work and resilience are just part of the culture there. But I also grew up watching the opportunity gap up close. I would see small businesses in my community that had real potential but no way to reach people beyond whoever already knew them. They could not afford a professional website. They could not afford to invest in their digital presence. And that gap between what they had and what they needed was not about effort. It was just about access.

That is what drove me to start WisdomLayers, my web development company, and to get involved with Code Forward, where I teach young people in my community how to actually build with technology. I did not want to wait until I had a degree to start making a difference. I wanted to start now.

0:55 to 1:30 — Show how the values show up in school and with others

In school, I show those values by being someone my peers can count on. As Secretary of the National Technical Honor Society I am not just filling a title. I am trying to make sure the people around me feel supported and seen. I share what I know. I help where I can. Integrity for me means doing that consistently, not just when it is convenient or when someone is watching.

Going into my engineering career, I want to design things the same way I try to live. With real people in mind. Technology that only benefits people who already have advantages is not really solving anything. My goal is to help build a future where innovation and equity go hand in hand. That is what making a positive difference actually looks like to me.
