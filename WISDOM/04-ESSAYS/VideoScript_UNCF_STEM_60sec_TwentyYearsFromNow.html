Twenty Years From Now

Wisdom M. Johnson | Pine Forest High School | Fayetteville, NC
UNCF STEM Scholars Prompt: Imagine yourself twenty years from now. What do you envision yourself doing, and how is it related to what you are learning now? How will your future work benefit your community, society, and the world? (60 seconds or less)
This is a spoken video script. Read it naturally, like you are talking to someone. Do not rush. 60 seconds at a relaxed pace is roughly 140 to 160 words. Practice until it feels like a conversation, not a recitation. Record in good light, look at the camera, and let your personality come through.
0:00 to 0:15 — Open with the vision

Twenty years from now, I am an engineer. Not just by title, but in the way that actually matters, building infrastructure in communities that have always had the need and rarely seen the investment.

0:15 to 0:40 — Connect it to right now

That future is a direct extension of what I am doing right now. Mechanical Engineering at NC A&T is teaching me how systems are designed, how they fail, and how to make them better. But the reason I care about that is something I learned before college even started. Growing up in Fayetteville, watching my mom counsel veterans at the VA, I understood early that the work you do either reaches people or it does not. And that gap is almost always a design decision someone made upstream.

0:40 to 0:60 — Close with the why

I want to be the engineer who makes that decision differently. For communities like the one I came from. That is not a plan I made up for this application. That is just where all of this has always been going.
