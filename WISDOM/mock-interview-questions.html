Post-Interview Questions

5 questions. ~1 minute each. Take notes, don't defend yourself.
5 min window
Don't explain yourself or defend your answers. Just ask, listen, and write down what they say. These 5 questions will give you everything you need.

Performance
On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate my overall performance — and what would it take to get to a 10?
⌄
Why this one
A number forces a real answer. The follow-up ("what would it take to get to a 10?") pushes them past the score and into exactly what's missing. If they say 7, you need to know what the 3 missing points look like.
2
~1 min
Strengths
What was the strongest moment of my interview, and what specifically made it stand out?
⌄
Why this one
Knowing what landed lets you repeat it. If they say "when you talked about your first client" — that's your anchor story going forward. Don't skip this one.
3
~1 min
Growth
If you could give me one thing to work on before my next real interview, what would it be?
⌄
Why this one
One thing — not five. When you ask for one they give you their most important feedback, not a list you can't act on. This is the most useful question on this page.
4
~1 min
Impression
What three words would you use to describe me based on this interview alone?
⌄
Why this one
Three words is a snapshot of how you're actually perceived — not how you think you came across. You might want to seem driven and confident, but they might say reserved or unsure. That gap matters.
5
~1 min
Next Steps
If you were the actual decision-maker, would I be a competitive candidate right now — and what's the gap between where I am and where I need to be?
⌄
Why this one
The honest closer. It forces a real answer about your competitiveness — not just "you did great." The gap they describe is exactly what you spend the next few weeks closing.
